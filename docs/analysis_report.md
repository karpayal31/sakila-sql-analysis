## Advanced Analysis

1. Customer Ranking Based on Total Spending

* Customers were ranked according to their total payment amount using window functions. This analysis helps identify high-value customers contributing the most revenue.
* A small group of customers contributes disproportionately to overall revenue.
* These customers represent potential VIP or loyalty program targets.

2. Running Revenue Trend Over Time

* A cumulative revenue calculation was performed using a running total over payment dates.
* SUM(amount) OVER (ORDER BY payment_date) used to compute cumulative revenue.
* Revenue growth shows a steady upward trajectory over time.
* Indicates consistent customer engagement and rental activity.

3. Top Movie Within Each Category

* The highest-earning movie in every category was identified using partitioned ranking.
* Calculated total revenue generated by each movie.
* Applied RANK() partitioned by category.
* Each genre has clear revenue-driving titles.
* Popular films vary significantly across categories.

4. Repeat Customer Behavior Analysis

Customer engagement was analyzed through spending patterns and rental frequency.

a) Total Spending Behavior

* Measured total payments made by each customer.
* Spending distribution shows distinct high and low spenders.
* Indicates opportunities for customer segmentation.
b) Purchase Frequency

* Calculated number of rentals per customer.
* Frequent renters demonstrate stronger platform engagement.
* High-frequency users are ideal candidates for subscription models.

c) Inactive Customer Detection

* Last rental dates were analyzed to identify inactive users.
* Customers with older rental dates may be at risk of churn.

5. RFM Analysis (Recency, Frequency, Monetary)

RFM metrics were calculated to evaluate customer value comprehensively.

**Metric**	**Meaning**
Recency		Last rental date
Frequency	Number of rentals
Monetary	Total spending

* Combines behavioral and financial indicators for accurate customer valuation.

6. Category Preference Analysis

* Rental counts were aggregated by movie category.
* Certain categories dominate customer demand.
* Reveals audience genre preferences.

7. Behavioral Segmentation

Customers were segmented into spending tiers:

High Value — Spending > 150
Medium Value — Spending between 75–150
Low Value — Spending < 75

*Clear customer tiers emerge based on purchasing behavior.

9. Rental Frequency Growth Analysis

* Customer rental growth trends were analyzed using cumulative window calculations and lag comparisons.
* Some customers show accelerating engagement over time.
* Growth-oriented customers indicate increasing loyalty.
* Early identification of emerging loyal customers.
* Opportunity for proactive retention strategies.

## Conclusion

The Sakila DVD Rental analysis provides a comprehensive understanding of revenue generation, customer behavior, film performance, and operational efficiency through SQL-driven analytics.

Overall, revenue performance is strongly influenced by customer spending patterns, category popularity, and seasonal demand trends. Mid-year months demonstrate peak business activity, while lower-performing periods indicate opportunities for targeted promotional strategies. Store-level comparisons reveal minor operational differences, with Store 2 achieving slightly higher revenue despite Store 1 maintaining a larger customer base.

Customer analytics show that a relatively small group of high-value customers contributes a significant share of total revenue, emphasizing the importance of retention and loyalty-focused initiatives. At the same time, rental frequency analysis highlights the presence of highly engaged customers whose behavior differs from high spenders, suggesting multiple customer personas within the business ecosystem.

Film and category analysis confirms that revenue concentration exists around specific genres—particularly Sports and popular high-performing titles—indicating that content strategy plays a crucial role in business success. Category-wise leaders further demonstrate how customer preferences vary across genres, reinforcing the value of data-driven inventory planning.

Advanced analytical techniques, including window functions, RFM analysis, behavioral segmentation, and growth trend evaluation, reveal deeper customer lifecycle insights. These analyses enable the identification of emerging loyal customers, inactive users at risk of churn, and distinct spending segments, supporting personalized marketing and long-term revenue optimization.

In conclusion, the analysis demonstrates how transactional rental data can be transformed into actionable business intelligence. By leveraging customer segmentation, targeted promotions, and data-informed inventory decisions, the DVD rental business can enhance customer retention, improve operational efficiency, and drive sustainable revenue growth.
